Redesigning Canada's favourite Coffee App

Role

  • UX Designer
  • UX Researcher

Project Type

  • Mobile and Web App Design

Tools Used

  • Miro
  • Adobe XD
  • Adobe Photoshop

Team

  • Lorenzo Queano - Sole Designer

Duration

  • Sep 2022 - Nov 2022

My class was tasked to pick out mobile apps that we do not like, whether it be based on design, experience and/or both. As a big coffee drinker, I knew how many times I wished the Tim Hortons app was redesigned. This context helped me a lot in what to redesign and how since its my own personal experience with the app, providing me a great headstart on getting insights about problems and possible solutions.

Once the research was done, I developed a style tile to use as a visual aid for my design. This involved choosing the right typography, colors, textures and imagery to convey the brand's message and capture its essence. I experimented with various designs and even tried my hand in reinventing the brand completely. After getting feedback from my colleagues and professor, I decided to continue with my true-to-brand original design.

The final step was to create wireframes (both mid- and high-fidelity) and execute the final web and mobile app design. This required advanced skills in graphic design software and an eye for detail. I considered the book's format, such as print or digital, and optimized the design for readability, visual appeal, and user experience. I also collaborated with the publisher and printers to ensure that the final product met the technical requirements and quality standards. Redesigning the book cover and layout for Treasure Island was a challenging but rewarding task that required creativity, research, and technical skills.
